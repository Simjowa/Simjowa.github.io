Gobal Warming and the Economy

Global warming is becoming more and more apparent as the years go by. Temperatures and sea levels have been rising and weather has been getting overall more extreme. These have started to have an effect on the global economy as a whole. Th agriculture sector of economics is greatly effected by climate change for example. As temperatures rise and change dramatically, this effects crops and crop rotation. Some crops that would normally be planted in early summer when things are still somewhat cool, now have much small and more unpredictable growth windows, making crop shortages much more abundant. These crop shortages can cause increases in the prices of some food items. One example is corn and soybeans, which are both used in a variety of products. If there were to be shortages in these items, the production of food that use things such as corn syrup and even animal feed will drop dramatically. These crops in particular are also used in the production of ethanol, used for gasoline. The changes in weather can also have an affect on cities infrastructure.
