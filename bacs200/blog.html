The Climate of Climate Change

A short blog discussing climate change

By: Simon Warga

Social Implications

We have already begun seeing social impacts coming from climate change. Things such as droughts and rising temperatures have been causing water shortages and threats to peoples livelihoods. This summer alone, Death Valley, California reached a temperature of 130 degrees F. Breaking the previous record it had set of 129 degrees last year, which was the highest temperature they had seen in over 90 years.

These drastic changes in temperature can also swing the other way, with extreme bouts of cold and snow. Last winter, Texas was hit with one of the worst winter storms they had ever experienced. They were hit with some of the lowest recorded temperatures the state had ever seen. It was because of this that most of their roads became completely unusable and their infrastructure began to completely collapse, resulting in the state's power grid shutting down causing mass power outages.

Economic Implications

Climate change can affect the economy in a variety of different ways. Some major impacts are:

  • Harsh temperatures affecting work environments and output
  • Adverse affects on the agricultural industry
  • Repair costs from damaged infrastructure and global clean up operations
  • Labor shortages due to harsh climate and working conditions
  • Increased flooding in coastal cities forcing extra investment in repair and prevention

Due to climate change, there have also been many research companies sprouting up that are dedicating research towards finding green alternatives to some things. A lot of investment has been put into renewable energy for example. Solar panel and wind turbine technology have been becoming more and more advanced as the years go on due to increased investment in their research and development. Not to say that climate change is a good thing, but without it there likely wouldn't have been as much investment into renewable energy alternatives.

Political Implications

Where we currently stand, public and political opinions are often very split on this subject. Some people believe we need to combat climate change and focus on placing laws and regulations that help combat it. Others believe that it is not a political priority and that there are other issues to focus on. On top of this, there is often small groups of people who believe that climate change isn't a problem at all, some of them going as far as to say that it doesn't exist.

Governments around the world have put different climate change policies in the hopes of combating some climate change. There are US policies such as The Clean Air Act, that focus on having states reduce their greenhouse gas emissions. There are also global policies and treaties such as the Paris Accords, that help to coordinate worldwide climate change efforts.

Conclusion and My Opinion

I have always believed that climate change is a huge problem that has been impacting us for a long time. As we get further along in this problem, it becomes more and more evident the impact that climate change is having on the world. Researchers have begun saying that we are at a big turning point with climate change, and if we continue on our trajectory, we may never be able to correct it. It's because of this, I think that we need to make combating climate change a priority. Prioritizing it can have economic and social impacts for sure, but as we are seeing now, if this continues the way it is it will impact the world in much worse ways.

In the end, there is no much one person can change. I just hope that more people can come to understand the severity of the threat we are facing. I also hope that people understand that, though at sometimes it may not seem bad, it's not just about us but those around us and future generations.
